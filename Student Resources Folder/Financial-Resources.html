What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a three-digit number, typically between 300 and 850, designed to represent your credit risk, or the likelihood you will pay your bills on time.

Here is a general look at credit score ranges:


  • 300-579: Poor
  • 580-669: Fair
  • 670-739: Good
  • 740-799: Very good
  • 800-850: Excellent

Why are credit scores important?

It’s important because it allows lenders to determine if you can handle your money correctly and if they should risk lending you money. Credit scores will enable you to do many like applying for a loan, car, house, etc.
